# DermaIQ: Survey Methodology and Findings

## Overview

This document presents the methodology and detailed findings from our market research surveys conducted between March and April 2025. These surveys were designed to validate the market need for DermaIQ and gather insights about consumer behavior regarding skincare ingredient transparency.

## Survey Methodology

### Locations and Timeline

We conducted in-person surveys at key UK beauty retailers:

1. **Sunday, March 16, 2025**
   - SpaceNK
   - Boots

2. **Saturday, March 22, 2025**
   - Boots
   - Selfridges

3. **Sunday, April 6, 2025**
   - Various locations

4. **Monday, April 7, 2025**
   - Various locations

### Participant Demographics

A total of 50+ participants were surveyed, with the following demographic breakdown:

- **Gender**: 85% female, 15% male
- **Age Range**: 18-55 years
- **Ethnicity**: Diverse representation including White British, South Asian, Middle Eastern, and other ethnicities
- **Occupation**: Students, professionals, and other consumers

### Survey Questions

Participants were asked a series of questions including:

1. How do you currently research skincare ingredients?
2. What sources do you trust for skincare information?
3. Do you use any apps or tools to help understand product ingredients?
4. What frustrates you about understanding skincare ingredients?
5. Would you be interested in an app that analyzes skincare ingredients?
6. Would you be willing to provide your email for updates on such an app?

## Key Findings

### Information Sources

| Source | Percentage of Respondents |
|--------|--------------------------|
| Google/Internet Searches | 42% |
| Social Media (Instagram, YouTube) | 28% |
| Existing Apps (Yuka, etc.) | 18% |
| Professional Advice | 12% |

### Consumer Pain Points

1. **Lack of Understanding**: 82% of respondents expressed difficulty understanding ingredient labels
2. **Information Overload**: 76% felt overwhelmed by the amount of information
3. **Conflicting Information**: 68% reported finding contradictory information about ingredients
4. **Time Consumption**: 65% said researching ingredients was too time-consuming
5. **Subscription Barriers**: 58% were frustrated by paywalls in existing apps

### Existing Solutions Mentioned

| Solution | Number of Mentions | Key Feedback |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Yuka | 5 | Limited free tier, not specialized for beauty products |
| INCI Beauty | 1 | Used by French consumers, not optimized for UK |
| ChatGPT | 3 | Used but not fully trusted for ingredient analysis |
| YouTube dermatologists | 4 | Trusted but time-consuming to watch |

### Interest in DermaIQ

- 90% showed interest in a reliable ingredient analysis tool
- 80% don't have a helpful resource for understanding ingredients
- 70% signed up for our waitlist by providing their email

## Interesting Observations

1. No respondents mentioned CosDNA (a popular ingredient analysis website in some markets)
2. Some participants asked if we were UCL students doing a project
3. Others thought we worked for the beauty retailers
4. Young white females were particularly interested in ingredient transparency
5. Multiple consumers mentioned using ChatGPT for ingredient research, indicating a growing trend
6. Several consumers expressed frustration with existing apps' limitations
7. Price sensitivity was noted, with most consumers preferring products under £25

## Market Segments Identified

Based on our survey responses, we identified four key market segments:

1. **Tech-Savvy Researchers**
   - Already using apps like Yuka or AI tools like ChatGPT
   - Frustrated with limitations of existing solutions
   - High potential for conversion to DermaIQ users

2. **Sensitive Skin Sufferers**
   - Actively avoiding specific ingredients
   - Currently struggling to decode ingredient lists
   - High willingness to try new solutions

3. **Passive Label Readers**
   - Check ingredients occasionally
   - Don't fully understand what they're reading
   - Need education and simplified information

4. **Brand Loyalists**
   - Trust established brands rather than researching ingredients
   - Potential to be converted through education
   - Lower initial conversion potential

## Waitlist Signups

The survey resulted in approximately 35 email signups for our waitlist, representing a 70% conversion rate from those surveyed. This strong initial interest validates the market demand for our solution.

## Conclusions and Recommendations

1. **Market Validation**: The survey strongly validates the need for DermaIQ, with 90% of respondents expressing interest.

2. **Feature Prioritization**:
   - Focus on ingredient education and simplified explanations
   - Include ingredient conflict identification (mentioned specifically by several respondents)
   - Ensure the free tier provides substantial value

3. **Marketing Strategy**:
   - Target young female consumers (showed highest interest)
   - Emphasize advantages over Yuka (most commonly mentioned competitor)
   - Highlight UK-specific optimization

4. **Pricing Strategy**:
   - Consider price sensitivity (£25 threshold mentioned)
   - Ensure free tier provides substantial value
   - Position premium features to justify subscription cost

5. **Next Steps**:
   - Develop MVP focusing on core features identified in survey
   - Conduct follow-up interviews with waitlist subscribers
   - Test pricing models with target segments
