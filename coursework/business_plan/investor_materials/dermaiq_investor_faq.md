# DermaIQ Investor FAQ

## Business Model & Market

### Q: What problem is DermaIQ solving?
**A:** DermaIQ addresses the significant information gap in skincare ingredient understanding. Our market research shows that 87% of consumers struggle to understand skincare ingredient labels, 92% are concerned about potentially harmful ingredients, and 76% would use an ingredient decoding app. This information gap creates anxiety, leads to poor purchasing decisions, and results in wasted money on ineffective or potentially harmful products.

### Q: How large is your target market?
**A:** The UK skincare market is valued at £2.8 billion (2025) and growing at 5.7% annually. The global skincare market is $189.3 billion (2025), growing at 6.2% annually. Our immediate target audience consists of 18.5 million UK consumers actively purchasing skincare products, with 68% willing to pay for personalized recommendations. The mobile skincare apps market is valued at £38 million in the UK and $1.2 billion globally, with significant growth projected as consumers increasingly rely on technology for skincare decisions.

### Q: What is your business model?
**A:** DermaIQ employs a freemium model with multiple revenue streams:

**Consumer Revenue:**
- **Free Tier**: Basic ingredient analysis, limited scans
- **Premium Tier (£3.99/month)**: Unlimited scans, personalized recommendations, routine tracking

**B2B Revenue:**
- **API Access**: For retailers and brands (£500-2,000/month)
- **White Label Solutions**: For beauty retailers (£2,000-5,000/month)
- **Data Insights**: Anonymized trend data for brands and retailers

**Additional Revenue:**
- Affiliate partnerships with recommended products
- Sponsored content from ethical brands

### Q: How will you acquire customers?

### Q: What is your pricing strategy?
**A:** Our pricing strategy is designed to maximize accessibility while ensuring sustainable revenue:

## Technology & Product

### Q: How does your technology work?
**A:** DermaIQ's technology combines three core components:

1. **Computer Vision**: Our proprietary computer vision technology scans product ingredient labels with 98% accuracy, extracting the complete ingredient list regardless of formatting or language.

2. **AI Analysis Engine**: Our AI analyzes each ingredient for safety, function, and potential concerns based on scientific research. It also considers ingredient interactions and concentrations.

3. **Personalization Algorithm**: Using the user's skin profile (created through an initial questionnaire and refined through feedback), our algorithm generates a personalized compatibility score and specific recommendations.

All components are built on scalable cloud infrastructure with machine learning capabilities that improve over time as more data is collected.

### Q: What is your technological advantage over competitors?
**A:** DermaIQ has several key technological advantages:

1. **Advanced Computer Vision**: Unlike competitors who rely on barcode scanning, our technology can analyze ingredient lists directly from product packaging, even for products not in our database.

2. **Comprehensive AI Analysis**: Our algorithm considers ingredient interactions and concentrations, not just individual ingredients in isolation.

3. **Sophisticated Personalization**: We analyze 20+ factors for personalization versus competitor average of 5-7 factors.

4. **Scientific Validation**: Our algorithms are developed in collaboration with UCL dermatology researchers, ensuring scientific accuracy.

5. **Cross-Platform Architecture**: Our technology is designed for seamless expansion across iOS, Android, and web platforms.

### Q: How accurate is your ingredient analysis?
**A:** Our ingredient recognition accuracy is 98% in various lighting conditions and packaging formats, significantly above the industry average of 85%. Our safety and compatibility assessments are based on peer-reviewed scientific research and validated by dermatology experts from UCL. We continuously update our database with the latest research findings and improve our algorithms through machine learning.

## Competition & Market Position

### Q: Who are your main competitors?
**A:** Our main competitors include:

1. **Think Dirty**: Mobile app that rates product safety by scanning barcodes
2. **EWG Healthy Living**: App by the Environmental Working Group that rates products based on potential health hazards
3. **INCI Decoder**: Website and app that breaks down ingredient lists with detailed explanations
4. **SkinSafe**: App developed with Mayo Clinic that helps users find products free from specific allergens
5. **Yuka**: Popular app that scans food and cosmetic products to provide health impact ratings

Indirect competitors include brand-specific apps like Neutrogena Skin360 and La Roche-Posay MySkinTrack.

### Q: How do you differentiate from competitors?
**A:** DermaIQ differentiates through seven key advantages:

1. **Advanced AI-Powered Ingredient Analysis**: Deeper, more accurate analysis than competitors
2. **Computer Vision Ingredient Recognition**: Direct label scanning versus barcode-only approaches
3. **Personalized Skin Compatibility Matching**: Truly personalized recommendations based on individual skin profiles
4. **Scientific Credibility & Transparency**: UCL partnership and evidence-based ratings
5. **Holistic Skin Health Integration**: Comprehensive approach beyond individual products
6. **Inclusive Design & Accessibility**: Designed for users of all backgrounds and abilities
7. **Ethical & Sustainable Product Discovery**: Helping users find products aligned with their values

No competitor currently offers the combination of computer vision technology, personalized recommendations, and scientific credibility that DermaIQ provides.

   - **Mitigation**: Phased development approach; early prototyping; partnerships with academic experts

2. **User Adoption**
   - **Risk**: Slower than projected user growth or premium conversion
   - **Mitigation**: Robust free tier; targeted marketing; continuous user feedback loops

3. **Competitive Response**
   - **Risk**: Increased competition or feature imitation from established players
   - **Mitigation**: Rapid innovation pace; patent protection; focus on building network effects

4. **Regulatory Changes**
   - **Risk**: Changes in data privacy regulations or ingredient classification standards
   - **Mitigation**: Privacy-by-design approach; regulatory monitoring; flexible data architecture

5. **Scaling Challenges**
   - **Risk**: Infrastructure or support issues with rapid user growth
   - **Mitigation**: Cloud-based scalable architecture; gradual rollout strategy; automated support systems
